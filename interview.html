Interview Scheduling

Self-scheduling experience for job candidates.

Overview

Before getting placed on assignment through EmployBridge, job candidates submit an application, have an interview with a recruiter, and do any required onboarding paperwork. EB is building a new platform for candidates to complete this application / interview / onboarding flow and I was responsible for designing the interview experience (scheduling, managing, cancelling, etc.) for this.

My Role

I was the sole designer on this project and was responsible for all aspects of the design from wireframing through handoff. I collaborated closely with a PM and engineers throughout this work.

Goals

The main business goal tied to this work is to increase interview show rate by 15%. The research team conducted discovery around the current interview scheduling experience including a survey with 57 respondants & user interviews to better understand what contributed to interview no-shows.

Design Process

Inspiration + early ideation

I started by reviewing the current process and analyzing scheduling experiences on other sites for inspiration. Then I jumped into sketching + low-fidelity wireframing intial ideas.

Integrating a vendor

To build this functionality, we integrated a third party scheduling vendor, TimeTap. It was unclear at first how custom we would be able to make the experience for first release. So I analyzed the out of the box experience from the vendor and customized it as much as possible using their tools, then documented changes our engineers would be able to make with CSS.

We ended up being able to build a custom experience, so I used the above screens & my initial wireframes as a starting place, incorporated our design system's components, and iterated based on feedback from the UX team.

Accounting for all scenarios

The interview scheduler ended up being a very simple UI - one screen where the user selects a date and time. However, this work had implications outside of that including - What should the experience be if there's a technical issue with the scheduling service? Where should information about the upcoming interview live within the experience? How will a user reschedule or cancel an interview? What if a user skips scheduling and tries to move on to other tasks? I created user flows to zoom out and visualize the holistic experience.

Research informed decisions

The research team's findings highlighted the need for all of the information about an upcoming interview to be easily accessible, and the rescheduling functionality to be easy to find.

Pushing our design system

Interview scheduling lives in between two other flows - the job application and the onboarding work. The entry point into the scheduling flow is the "application success" screen - after a user submits a job application, they are taken to a screen letting them know their application is in and prompting them to schedule their interview. After their interview is scheduled, the user is taken to a second success screen prompting them to continue to onboarding.

The success screens are meant to add pause to the flow, display any relevant information to the user, and encourage them to continue on to the next step. The design for these needed to have a pattern that would be flexible enough to work for different use cases. I explored different designs, playing around with hierarchy and information architecture.

After rounds of design feedback, I landed on the designs below. I worked with engineering to create a custom component (based on our existing stepper component) for our system.

Celebrating the user

As we showed users where they were at in the process, we also wanted to provide encouragement by celebrating the steps they completed. I achieved this by incorporating emojis, encouraging copy, and adding in a confetti animation.

Results

This work has shipped, is currently live in 5 EB branches, and is continuing to roll out. The goal of creating this streamlined scheduling experience is to increase interview show rate by 15% (+900 interviews per week). Data is still being collected on that, but early success indicators show that interviews scheduled have increased 10% and 92% of candidates that create an account go on to schedule an interview.
